Key Achievements for the Quarter
  • 365000 farming households have been through radio extension, one on one farm activities and group and cooperative coaching and training on conservation and climate smart agriculture, post -harvest management and business skills and marketing, livelihoods and resilience development.
  • 3456 children have been supported through our children corner, community based child care center, teen clubs and after library services.
  • 31800 women, children, men and young adults have been supported through mobile and outreach health services, with 1234 defaulters traced back into treatment, care and support.
  • 546 young people have accessed ICT, vocational and technical training, business and life skills through youth programing.
  • 123456 learners in primary schools have accessed quality Comprehensive Sexuality Education through enhanced life skills education programing with school governance structures.

"IMPROVED HOUSEHOLD LIVELIHOOD THROUGH AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTIVITY"
Meet Jabili Kamanga, 70 years of age , a husband, Father of 4 and a grandfather of 6 children. He comes from Village Headman Chunga in Katengeza. He is a member of Katengeza Pre Cooperative. He is one of the members who have improved household livelihood through farming and VSL. He has been a farmer for quite a long time but he couldn’t have enough food for his household due to lack of technical support in his farming.

"NO WEALTH WITHOUT GOOD HEALTH"
Access to health care remains a burden in rural Malawi with pregnant and lactating women walking more than 50KM to the nearest health facility. FOCCADs approach to health bridges the transport and logistics gap between communities and health facilities through door to door and outreach health services and provision of emergency transport services to the most vulnerable and needy clients to health facilities.

"MY PERFORMANCE HAS GREATLY IMPROVED IN CLASS"
Tiyanjane Dausi, a standard 7 and 14-year-old Leaner at St Paul’s Primary School in Nkhotakota District in Malawi is a practical example of work in progress in ensuring girls achieve their dreams in society. The journey to supporting girl child education and growth is taking good shape in Malawi through FOCCAD programing on comprehensive sexuality education and child corner. Guarding girls against early forced marriages, ensuring girls are retained in school is our long term mandate. After school programing keeps engaging children into learning and planning for life.

"Women empowerment through Self Help Group"
While there are several strategies supporting women empowerment around the globe, the Self Help Group approach stands out in supporting and training women beyond savings. FOCCAD adopted the SHG approach with support from Kindernot Hilfe (KNH) through over 40 clubs with membership of 1300 women who testifies of enhanced skills in savings, business, leadership and group dynamics.Our story leads to Martha Kadewere aged 28, married with 5 children who comes from Nkomakachoka village T.A Malengachanzi in Nkhotakota district.
